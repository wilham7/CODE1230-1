After running the script and getting results it came to our understanding that there would be multiple fail cases in which the results would be skewed or not portrayed correctly. Our script would work as intended if the two ends of a dataset were either; a. Distinctly separate or b. Interwoven

However, there are many cases where finding the midpoint is not an effective solution as it would produce misleading results. This could include both a failure to detect differences simply because the midpoints were aligned as well as unusually calculated midpoints like in the case of isolated clusters.
